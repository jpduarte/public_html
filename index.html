Hi, I am Juan!

I received my B.Sc.(2010) and M.Sc.(2012) degrees from the Department of Electrical Engineering, Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST), Daejeon, Korea, in 2010 and 2012 respectively. There, I had the pleasure to work with professor Yang-Kyu Choi and his Nobelab group. I am actually born and raised in Chile, indeed, after my Master in South Korea, I hold positions as a lecturer at Universidad Tecnica Federico Santa Maria, Valparaiso, Chile, in 2012, in the Department of Electronic Engineering and the Department of Physics, respectively. I am currently working toward my PhD. degree in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at the University of California, Berkeley, where I am part of BSIM Group. During 2016, I have been a Graduate Student Instructor for the course "EE16B: Designing Information Devices and Systems II" for spring and fall semesters.

In BSIM group, I work with professor Chenming-Hu, where we develop physics-based, accurate, scalable, robust, and predictive MOSFET SPICE models for circuit simulation and CMOS technology development. Our models are used by major semiconductor manufacturers and circuit designers. I also collaborate with professor Sayeef Salahuddin in the development of models for Negative-Capacitance FETs.

During my time as a researcher, I have been the author or coauthor of more than 50 papers related to nanoscale semiconductor devices and bio-sensors. In 2013, I received the Best Student Paper Award at the 2013 International Conference on Simulation of Semiconductor Processes and Devices (SISPAD) for the paper "Unified FinFET Compact Model: Modelling Trapezoidal Triple-Gate FinFETs". My paper entitled "Compact Models of Negative-Capacitance FinFETs: lumped and distributed charge models" was accepted into IEDM and presented on December 7th at San Francisco. You can also find a list of my publications here.

During 2017, I lectured a DeCal course called Hardware Makers. The ultimate purpose of the course was to have fun with hardware! Students learned how to create hardware with wifi/bluetooth connections of several prototyping boards, sensors, actuators, and additional electronics components.

In my free time, I enjoy riding bikes around California and Chile, you can follow my cycling activities in strava. I am part of Cal Cycling, where I race for the category A team. In May 2016, our team got the 5th place in Collegiate Nationals Time Trial Race. In April 2017 I was 16th in the general omnium of Cycling Collegiate Nationals at Grand Junction, Colorado. I am very concerned about the amount of trash in the beautiful routes of California and the wold in general, so with some friends we have founded the Cyclists Green Initiative. Our main goal is to pick up a piece of trash for each cycling ride. It is incredible the amount of trash that we have collected in such a short time, if you want to join us, just start picking up some trash while biking!
